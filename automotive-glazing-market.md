<p><p>The automotive glazing market consists of three primary types: laminated glass, tempered glass, and polycarbonate. Laminated glass features a plastic interlayer between two glass sheets, enhancing safety and sound insulation. Tempered glass is heat-treated for increased strength and shatter resistance, making it ideal for side and rear windows. Polycarbonate, a lightweight plastic alternative, offers high impact resistance and flexibility, commonly used in specialized applications like armored vehicles and sports cars. Each type serves distinct safety, performance, and aesthetic needs.</p></p>
