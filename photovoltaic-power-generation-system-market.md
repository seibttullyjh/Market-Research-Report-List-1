<p><p>Photovoltaic power generation systems can be categorized into three main types. Stand-alone solar photovoltaic systems operate independently, providing power in remote areas without grid access. Grid-connected systems are integrated with the electricity grid, allowing for energy exchange and utilization of solar power while retaining grid stability. Distributed solar photovoltaic systems are installed close to the point of consumption, such as homes or businesses, promoting localized energy generation and reducing transmission losses. Each type plays a crucial role in advancing renewable energy adoption.</p></p>
