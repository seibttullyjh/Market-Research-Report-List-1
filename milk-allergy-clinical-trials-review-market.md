<p><ul><li>Skin Prick Tests</li><li>Blood Allergen Specific IgE Ttests</li><li>Oral Food Challenge</li><li>Food Elimination Diet</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Milk allergy clinical trials involve various testing methods to assess allergic reactions. Skin prick tests involve introducing allergen extracts into the skin to observe reactions, offering quick results. Blood allergen-specific IgE tests measure the immune system's response to milk proteins via blood samples. Oral food challenges involve administering controlled amounts of milk to monitor reactions in a clinical setting. Food elimination diets entail removing milk from the diet to identify symptom relief and confirm allergies through subsequent reintroduction testing.</p></p>
